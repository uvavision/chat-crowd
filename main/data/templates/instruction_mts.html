Task Descriptions
  1. We have a simple quiz for the first-time participants. It is mainly for preparing you for the task.
  2. After you login (or pass the quiz), you will see the chat interface. Please wait briefly for the user to join you.
  3. Please start interacting with the user once you are notified via the chat interface and email.
  4. Everytime after user sends new content. Please click [2] to get the analysis results from VA. The analysis includes the response [4] and the value for the fields such as [6].
  5. Please help correct VA's analysis by adding/removing/modifying form content, such as [6].
  6. Please help improve VA's natural language response when it doesn't help address user's intend. Edit or rewrite on [4], copy with [5] and paste to [1] to sent to the user.
  7. You can also use the form (Rental Information) to search for the apartments. Please note that for certain field such as "neighborhood", you need to select from the auto-complete options.
  8. Please only end the conversation after confirming with the other user.
  9. Please note that you need to click [5] to end the task.
  10. After you end the task, you will be provided a code to fill the blank at the very bottom of this page to complete the task.
How to do a good job?
Do's Dont's
Please use the search tool to look for the information based on user's intent. Do NOT forge information in your response to user.
Please confirm or clarify with user about requests if necessary. When the request is not in task scope, e.g., "I prefer new building", please tell the user that the building year information is not available.
Please explain why you suggest certain things if necessary. Don't change the search criteria without informing the user.
Please make your response relevant, succint and useful to address the user' requests. Don't involve inconsequential conversations or any personal information.
Try to have a conversation in the range of 10~15 utterances (if you say 5 lines and they say 5 lines that is 10 utterances total). Don't write a few lines and end the conversation.
A Good Example
user: Hi, I am looking for rental apartment.
YOU: Please tell me your preferences on location. (Asking for preferences.)
user: So, preferably midtown Manhattan or Brooklyn Heights
YOU: I will look for apartments in Midtown Manhattan for now. (Telling the user what you are doing.)
user: OK.
YOU: What is your price range and how many bedrooms are you looking for? (Asking for additional preferences to narrow the search.)
user: one-bed, between $1500 and $2900. prefer big windows.
YOU: Sorry that we cannot search by "big windows". Now searching one-bedroom apartments for you between $1499 and $2999. (Telling the user what you can do.)
user: Oh OK thanks.
YOU: Over 200 apartments in over 90 buildings. Do you have other preferences like subway lines or number of baths?
user: An apartment near line 2 would be great.
YOU: Okay, I see 50 apartments that meet your criteria. I think this one would be a good fit for you: 1600 BROADWAY, #3917 (Midtown, Manhattan) $2800; studio; 1 s, 592 square feet. (Suggesting an apartment.)
user: Actually I think I might want something a little cheaper than that.
YOU: Okay, how about this one: 5 BRYANT PARK #2613 (Midtown, Manhattan) $2500; studio; 1 bath (Adjusting to new information.)
user: That looks great, thanks!
YOU: You're welcome. Bye!
A Bad Example
user: Hi, I am looking for rental apartment.
YOU: Hey, how you doing? Where are you from? (Avoid unnecessary chitchat.)
user: I'm good, Michigan.
YOU: Please tell me your preferences on location and price range.
user: So, preferably midtown Manhattan or Brooklyn Heights and under $800
YOU: There are no apartments that match those criteria. Sorry! I can't help you. (Please help modify search criteria to explore other options.)
user: Okay. Thanks, bye.
