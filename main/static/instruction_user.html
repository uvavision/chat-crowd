  • There is a simple quiz for the first-time participants. It is mainly for preparing you for the task. After you login (or pass the quiz), you will see the chat interface.
  1. IMPORTANT When you specify your initial preferences on the rental apartments, please use the pre-assigned initial preferences in [1]. These could be adjusted later but the conversation should be started based on [1].
  2. Step 1: Review the chat history in area [2]. Determine what to do next [3].
  3. Step 2:
    • You could take your turn to submit messages to continue the conversation. As shown in the example, a proper turn of message is shown in the typing area []. Then press enter to submit your message.
    • Or you can click "end" button [] to end the conversation, if,
      1. you have achieved your goal - possible options found.
      2. you have explored your options with the agent and the agent is not able to find an apartment meeting your criteria.

How to do a good job?
Do's Dont's
Please make your message relevant, succint and useful for finding a task. English only. Please do NOT involve uncommon slangs/typos, inconsequential conversations or any personal information.
Please understand your pre-defined preferences and use your commonsense. Do NOT make out-of-scope requests.
Please understand the service is limited. Please do not quit the task without exploring the service by the agent.
A Good Example
YOU: Hi, I am looking for rental apartment.
agent: Please tell me your preferences on location.
YOU: So, preferably midtown Manhattan or Brooklyn Heights. (Giving preferences.)
agent: Good news. There are 20 apartments available in midtown Manhattan.
YOU: OK.
agent: What is your price range and how many bedrooms are you looking for?
YOU: one-bed, between $1500 and $2900. prefer big windows. (Answering with additional preferences.)
agent: Sorry that we cannot search by "big windows". Now searching one-bedroom apartments for you between $1499 and $2999.
YOU: Oh OK. Good to know.
agent: Over 200 apartments in over 90 buildings. Do you have other preferences like subway lines or number of baths?
YOU: An apartment near line 2 would be great. (Answering the agent with more information.)
agent: I think this one would be a good fit for you: 1600 BROADWAY, #3917 (Midtown, Manhattan) $2800; studio; 1 s, 592 square feet.
YOU: Actually I think I might want something a little cheaper than that. (Asking the the agent for the options to your preference.)
agent: Okay, how about this one: 5 BRYANT PARK #2613 (Midtown, Manhattan) $2500; 1 beds; 1 bath
YOU: That looks great, thanks! (Accepting an apartment.)
agent: You're welcome. Anything else I can help with?
user: Not now. Thanks. Bye! (END THE CONVERSATION)
A Bad Example
YOU: Hi, where are you from? (Avoid random chitchat.)
agent: I'm from Michigan. How can I help you?
YOU: I am looking for rental apartment.
agent: Please tell me your preferences on location.
YOU: I like buildings with glass window (Please try to respond to agent's question.)
agent: What is your price range?
YOU: less than $100 (Please make your request base on the commonsense)
agent: There are no apartments in that price range but I can look for some other cheaper options.
YOU: Never mind then, bye! (Please adjust search criteria to explore other options.)
